# Caseworker usability test

This protocol is for moderated testing with real housing caseworkers. Never use real client records in a test environment.

## Participants

Recruit 5-8 caseworkers with a mix of intake, document-review, and application-submission experience. Obtain informed consent and compensate participants according to the organization’s research policy.

## Scenario

Use the synthetic Jordan Rivera case. Ask each participant to:

1. Upload a PDF and a phone photo, then explain what the extraction status means.
2. Approve one extracted value, correct one, and reject one while identifying its source evidence.
3. Resolve the application’s remaining questions without opening the full form.
4. Add a second job with a non-monthly pay frequency and explain the resulting monthly total.
5. Capture electronic consent and signature, generate the application, and select supporting documents.
6. Submit for review, switch to the reviewer role, approve it, and identify the configured delivery destination.
7. As an administrator, import a test AcroForm, map two fields, publish it, and create a new version.

## Measures

- Task completion without moderator rescue.
- Time on task and navigation reversals.
- Incorrect extraction approvals or unintended document disclosures.
- Whether the participant can explain source provenance, confidence, template version, signature status, and delivery status.
- System Usability Scale score and one open-ended “most confusing moment” response.

## Stop conditions

Stop a task if the participant would disclose the wrong document, approve a value they believe is uncertain, or cannot recover after three minutes. Record the reason without coaching through the decision.

## Acceptance criteria

- At least 80% unassisted completion for each safety-critical task.
- Zero unintended document disclosures.
- Every participant correctly identifies that extraction is proposed until reviewed.
- Median System Usability Scale score of at least 75.

Record findings in a dated research report. Do not mark real-user testing complete until actual participant sessions have occurred.
